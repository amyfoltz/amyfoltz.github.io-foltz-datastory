My Data Story: By Amy Foltz

My data project highlighted personal messaging trends between a group of roommates on Facebook Messenger.

I have created a visualization of messaging topics between four people over the course of five days.

The dynamics of the group's conversations affect many aspects of the individuals' lives. The different colored lines represent different topics of conversation; the type of line denotes what kind of message was sent (text, emoji, picture, etc.). The thickness of the lines indicates its relative importance (on a scale of 1-10).

This video examines the ways Facebook has access to its users' conversations.

Facebook keeps track of the topics its users talk about using different algorithms that search for key words or phrases.

Facebook can sell this information to advertizing companies, who use the information to design advertisements for each user's profile. There a few ways to limit the amount of information advertisers have access to, which are highlighted in my video.
